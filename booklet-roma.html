III.
Setting the time

Three positions. One crown.

The crown sits at three o'clock and has two pulled positions. The watch is always running while the crown is in position 0.

Pushed in.
Normal wear. Water-resistant.
One click.
Date set. Rotate clockwise.
Fully out.
Time set. Seconds pause.

Always return the crown to position 0 after setting. Never pull the crown when the watch is wet.

IV.
Water resistance

5 ATM. Read this carefully.

The Roma is rated to 5 ATM, equivalent to roughly 50 metres of static water pressure. This rating describes resistance, not depth of use. The figure assumes a still watch on a still wrist.

    Suitable for

  • Light rain
  • Hand-washing
  • Splashes at a sink

    Not suitable for

  • Showering
  • Swimming
  • Diving or snorkelling
  • Hot tubs or saunas

Hot water, soap, and steam degrade the seals. Damage caused by water exposure beyond the stated rating is not covered by the warranty.

V.
Daily wear & care

How to keep it well.

  • CleaningWipe the case and bracelet with the supplied microfiber cloth. For build-up between links, a soft toothbrush with water works.
  • MagnetismQuartz movements are more resilient than mechanical, but avoid placing the watch against speaker magnets or laptops for long periods.
  • TemperatureAvoid extreme heat (saunas, dashboards in summer) and rapid temperature changes.
  • ChemicalsPerfume, sunscreen, salt water and cleaning agents will accelerate wear on the seals and finish. Apply scent before the watch goes on.
  • StorageReturn the watch to its presentation case when not worn. It is the only environment we made specifically for it.

VI.
Sizing the bracelet

Removing a link.

The Roma's integrated bracelet is sized by pushing connecting pins out of the link. The link-removal pin supplied with the watch fits the channel. A small hammer or firm, steady pressure is required.

  1. I.Identify the arrow on the underside of the bracelet. Pins push out in the direction of the arrow.
  2. II.Place the bracelet on a stable surface. Hold the supplied pin against the connecting pin.
  3. III.Press or tap until the pin emerges, then grip with pliers and pull through.
  4. IV.Reattach by pressing the pin back the opposite way until flush.

If unsure, any jeweller can size an integrated bracelet in under five minutes.

VII.
The battery

When it stops.

The Miyota precision quartz movement runs on a small silver-oxide cell. Expected life is approximately 2 to 3 years of continuous wear, depending on use.

Battery replacement should be carried out by a qualified watchmaker. Opening the caseback without the correct tools will compromise the gaskets and void the water resistance rating.

Battery replacement is not covered by the warranty. It is normal consumable wear.

IX.
Warranty

Two years. International.

What is covered.

Manufacturing defects in materials and workmanship for a period of two years from the date of original purchase. Movement faults, case defects, dial printing faults, bracelet structural defects.

What is not.

Normal wear and ageing, accidental impact, water damage beyond the rated resistance, unauthorised modification or repair, and consumables such as battery replacement.
